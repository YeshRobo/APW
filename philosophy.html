What I Mean by Agent

An agent is anything that can perceive the current state of its environment, process that perception, and act to change a system.

The important part is the loop. The agent does not only observe, and it does not only act. It connects perception, internal processing, and action.

Why Agents Exist at Many Levels

A control system, robot, software system, human, or organization can all be viewed as an agent when it senses conditions, processes information, and acts on the world.

The intelligence level changes, but the loop remains useful: perceive, process, act.

Current System State → Changed System State

What the Agent Core Does

The Agent Core is the decision-making center of the agent. It receives perception, forms an internal representation of the current system state, and selects or produces the next action.

In engineering terms, the Agent Core can be a controller, policy, planner, retrieval pipeline, reasoning process, or decision structure.

Examples

Gimbal

A control algorithm converts IMU and encoder feedback into motor commands.

RL Car

A learned policy selects steering and motion actions while respecting safety constraints.

Library Agent

A retrieval and organization system turns scattered documents into searchable structure.

Human

The mind receives perception, reasons about the situation, and selects action.

Organization

People, rules, plans, communication, and decision structure coordinate action at larger scale.

Why This Matters for Robotics, AI, and Systems Engineering

This framing makes engineering projects easier to compare. A robot, an embedded controller, a software analytics system, and a document agent can all be explained by what they perceive, how they process it, what action they take, and what system state changes.

That is the lens I use across my portfolio: build systems that connect perception, decision-making, action, and measurable outcomes.
